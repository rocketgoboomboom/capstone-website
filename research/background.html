Background Research

What are anomalies? How is it different in NASA's environment? We started with a blank slate, knowing little about NASA and the problem at hand. We went through various stages of extensive background research to set a solid foundation of understanding.

Literature Review

Understanding NASA Human Systems Integration Division, training procedures, and creative problem solving

NASA Interviews

Interviewing astronauts, spaceflight trainers, researchers, and flight controllers

Analogous Domains

Interviewing pilots, new parents, game designers, scientists, and designers to understand

Creative Problem Solving

Historical understanding of anomalies and the qualities that made them difficult to solve

Literature Review

Currently, NASA is researching new ways to aid astronauts in solving anomalies in space, but there is no standard method to assess readiness for their nearest furtherest mission: going to Mars.

Our goal is to empower researchers to better identify gaps in anomaly resolution through evaluating teams' performances in creative problem solving scenarios. To achieve this, we began researching extensively in relevant areas.
